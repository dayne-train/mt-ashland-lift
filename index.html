The community

Mt. Ashland is not a resort. It's a non-profit mountain owned by the community it serves, kept open by the people who learned to ski here, met their friends here, taught their kids here. For more than four decades, the Ariel and Windsor chairlifts have been part of that story, carrying first-timers up the bunny hill and powder chasers up to the bowl.

But four decades is a long time for any machine. Both lifts have grown outdated, increasingly costly to maintain, and less efficient than what modern winters demand. We can keep patching, or we can build the next 45 years.

The board chose to build. The Elevate Mt. Ashland campaign is a capital push to replace both chairlifts, so the next generation has the same access to winter adventure, learning, and joy that this one had.
